Automatic Knowledge Graph population from Social Big Data in the Political Marketing Domain

Social media have become platforms for conversation and channels to share experiences and opinions, promoting public discourse and, in particular, increasingly used for political context, such as citizen participation, proselytism or political discussions. One of the most important and essential activities from political marketing is the collect, monitor, processing and analyze a large amount of data for the design of successful campaigns that impact the citizens. However, the extraction, integration, processing and storage of these torrents of data relevant in the political domain is a very challenging endeavor. In the last few years, ontologies, knowledge graphs (KGs) and semantic technologies have proven effective in knowledge support extracting and management with a single data integration, helping in the complexity of data relationship and solving data heterogeneity problem. Our proposal focuses on providing a (semi-) automated solution for gathering significant data from semi-structured and unstructured digital media sources to feed a knowledge graph previously defined in ontological RDF schema for the political marketing domain.
